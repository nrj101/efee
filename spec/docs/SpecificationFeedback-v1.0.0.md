## Design review outcome

The business intent is strong, but the design baseline is not yet internally consistent enough for deterministic later-stage development. I found six decisions that should be resolved before treating the architecture, ATS, or persistence models as frozen. I reviewed documents only—no implementation findings are included.

| Blocking area | Design discrepancy | Clarification needed |
|---|---|---|
| Financial truth | BR-011 and the domain model define outstanding balance as derived from obligation lines, discounts, and settlements; Fee Obligation ATS/persistence instead make it independently mutable and declare no derived state. | Define the single source of truth and whether `outstandingAmount` is a calculated value, a rebuildable cache, or neither. |
| Discount model | Architecture defines `Discount Policy → Discount Grant → Applied Discount`; the ATS/persistence model defines one direct, student-specific `Discount`. | Choose one canonical model, then align ownership, terminology, state, lifecycle, and repositories. |
| Financial effect entities | Obligation Line, Payment Allocation, and Applied Discount are essential software concepts, but lack complete state, lifecycle, APIs, and persistence contracts. | Define these entities and their identifiers, immutable facts, relationships, and correction/reversal behaviour. |
| Policy applicability | The business/domain model says Fee Structures are versioned policies for a group of students in an Academic Year; the technical/persistence model holds only name, components, and `active`. | Define how a policy is selected for a student, tied to an Academic Year, approved/published, versioned, and snapshotted into an obligation. |
| Audit, approvals, corrections | MVP and Business Rules require approvals, correction history, and full traceability, but no design owns or persists the required facts. | Define an append-only audit/correction model, including actor, time, reason, approver, original fact, and corrective fact. |
| MVP coverage | Financial Corrections and Reporting are in scope, but have no module/service contract, read-model design, or persistence strategy. | Add application-service and query/reporting contracts; corrections need a designated workflow owner. |

The most consequential conflict is financial correctness. [Business Rules](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/spec/docs/BusinessRules.md:137>) and the [Software Domain Model](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/spec/docs/SoftwareDomainModel.md:215>) require obligation-line-level facts and derived totals. In contrast, [Fee Obligation ATS](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/technical-specification/aggregates/FeeObligation.md:53>) and its [persistence model](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/technical-design/persistence/FeeObligation.md:64>) expose mutable aggregate-level outstanding amount without defining the underlying line, settlement, or discount application records. That prevents reliable reconciliation, audit reproduction, payment allocation, and correction handling.

The Discount area has two incompatible designs. [Aggregate Design](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/architecture/AggregateDesign.md:188>) uses a policy/grant/applied-effect model; [Discount ATS](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/technical-specification/aggregates/Discount.md:109>) uses a single direct discount. Neither fully captures the mandated reason, approver, and supporting comments from [BR-023](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/spec/docs/BusinessRules.md:236>). Pick one model before expanding the implementation.

Other high-priority ambiguities:

- Lifecycle models are insufficient or contradictory. Student ATS has Registered → Active → Inactive, but persistence uses one Boolean. Academic Year must preserve a Fee Structure association, but its persistence model explicitly has no relationships. Payment’s “recorded,” “accepted,” and “realised” states are not defined, even though allocation depends on realisation. [Student ATS](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/technical-specification/aggregates/Student.md:71>) · [Academic Year ATS](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/technical-specification/aggregates/AcademicYear.md:38>) · [Payment rules](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/spec/docs/BusinessRules.md:157>)

- Academic-Year closure requires a Final Settlement Obligation, but that concept is absent from the glossary, domain model, aggregate catalogue, and technical/persistence designs. [BR-003](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/spec/docs/BusinessRules.md:75>)

- Fee Components are owned by Fee Structure, yet Discount ATS stores a `List<FeeComponent>`. This conflicts with the architecture’s rule that entities cannot be shared between aggregates. Decide whether downstream aggregates retain component IDs or immutable snapshots. [Aggregate ownership rules](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/architecture/AggregateDesign.md:185>) · [Discount ATS](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/technical-specification/aggregates/Discount.md:113>)

- Workflow documents introduce rules that do not exist in Business Rules—for example, “only one Academic Year may be active” and “every accepted payment shall be acknowledged by a receipt.” Since workflows say Business Rules are authoritative, promote, reject, or explicitly classify these requirements. [Fee Policy workflow](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/spec/docs/workflows/FeePolicyManagement.md>) · [Receipt workflow](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/spec/docs/workflows/ReceiptManagement.md>)

- Cross-cutting architecture requires separate system IDs and business references, including existing receipt numbering, but technical/persistence models use one ambiguous String identifier. They also lack the audit metadata the cross-cutting policy requires. [Cross-cutting concerns](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/architecture/CrossCuttingConcerns.md:86>)

- Governance status is inconsistent: [StatusReport](</D:/Study/Projects/Student Fee Receivables Platform(efee)/project/StatusReport.md:5>) calls the technical baseline approved, while Software Architecture, Aggregate Design, every ATS, and every persistence model remain `Draft`. The later documents should not be described as frozen until their contradictions are resolved and their status/version updated.

Recommended next move: create a short design-reconciliation package before further implementation—one ADR for the receivables ledger/derivation model, one for discounts, one for policy/enrolment relationships, and one for audit/approvals/corrections. Then update all affected artifacts together and approve a consistent baseline.
